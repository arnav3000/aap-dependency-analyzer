## Use GPL v3 (Free) When

✅ **Personal Projects**

- Learning and experimentation
- Personal automation tools
- Home lab environments

✅ **Academic & Research**

- University coursework
- Research projects
- Educational demonstrations
- Academic publications

✅ **Open Source Projects**

- Contributing to GPL-compatible open source
- Building tools that will be open-sourced
- Non-profit community projects

✅ **Internal Business Tools (with conditions)**

- Internal-only use (not distributed)
- You're comfortable with GPL copyleft
- You don't mind releasing modifications if distributed

✅ **Evaluation & Testing**

- Proof of concept
- Technology evaluation
- Development and testing environments

---

## Need Commercial License When

💼 **Proprietary Software**

- Embedding in closed-source products
- Integration with proprietary systems
- Cannot release source code due to business requirements

💼 **SaaS & Managed Services**

- Offering this tool as a service to customers
- Multi-tenant platforms
- Managed AAP migration services
- Consulting services using this tool

💼 **Commercial Distribution**

- Selling products that include this tool
- OEM/bundled software
- White-label solutions
- Reselling or redistributing

💼 **Enterprise Requirements**

- Need warranty and indemnification
- Require support SLAs
- Legal compliance concerns with GPL
- Risk management policies prohibit GPL

💼 **Custom Development**

- Need features not in open source version
- Require vendor support and maintenance
- Want influence over product roadmap

## Common Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Internal Tool at Enterprise

**Question:** "We want to use this for internal AAP migrations. Do we need commercial license?"

**Answer:**

- **GPL v3 OK if:**
  - Tool is only used internally (not distributed to customers/partners)
  - You're comfortable with GPL terms
  - Legal/compliance team approves GPL software
  
- **Commercial License if:**
  - Your company policy prohibits GPL software
  - You need warranty/indemnification
  - You want priority support
  - You need legal protection

**Recommendation:** Start with GPL v3 for evaluation, switch to commercial if legal/compliance requires it.

---

### Scenario 2: SaaS Migration Platform

**Question:** "We're building a SaaS platform for AAP migrations. Can we use GPL?"

**Answer:** **No, you need a Commercial License.**

**Why:** Offering software as a service to customers = distribution. GPL would require your entire SaaS platform to be GPL-licensed.

**License Needed:** Standard or Enterprise commercial license

---

### Scenario 3: Consulting Services

**Question:** "We provide AAP consulting. Can we use this tool with clients?"

**Answer:** **Depends on how you use it.**

- **GPL v3 OK if:**
  - You run the tool yourself for analysis
  - Clients don't get the software
  - You deliver reports/recommendations only
  
- **Commercial License if:**
  - You install/deploy the tool for clients
  - Clients get access to the software
  - You charge for the tool specifically

**Recommendation:** Commercial license gives you more flexibility and legal protection.

---

### Scenario 4: ISV Embedding

**Question:** "We sell an AAP management platform. Can we embed this?"

**Answer:** **No under GPL. Yes with Commercial/OEM License.**

**Why:** Embedding in a commercial product = distribution. GPL would force your entire product to be GPL.

**License Needed:** OEM/Embedded commercial license with white-label rights

---

### Scenario 5: Academic Research

**Question:** "Using this for my PhD research on automation platforms. License?"

**Answer:** **GPL v3 (Free) ✅**

**Why:** Academic research is explicitly permitted under GPL.

**Note:** If results become a commercial product later, commercial license needed then.

## GPL v3 Explained (Simple Terms)

**What GPL v3 allows:**

- ✅ Use the software for any purpose
- ✅ Modify the source code
- ✅ Distribute copies
- ✅ Distribute modified versions

**What GPL v3 requires:**

- 📜 Include GPL license with distribution
- 📜 Provide source code when you distribute
- 📜 Derivative works must also be GPL
- 📜 Can't add additional restrictions

**The "copyleft" rule:**
If you distribute modified versions, they must also be GPL v3. This means:

- Your modifications become open source
- Your combined work must be GPL-licensed
- Users get same freedoms you had

**This is why commercial software often cannot use GPL.**

---

## Commercial License Explained

**What you get:**

- ✅ Use in proprietary/closed-source software
- ✅ No requirement to open-source modifications
- ✅ No GPL copyleft obligations
- ✅ Warranty and indemnification (Enterprise)
- ✅ Support and maintenance
- ✅ Legal protection

**What you pay for:**

- Per-organization annual fee
- Support costs
- Custom development (optional)

**Think of it as:**

- GPL = Free but with rules (copyleft)
- Commercial = Paid but with freedom (no copyleft)
